After one of the tightest winters in recent memory, something shifted in April. New listings in the Twin Cities metro are up roughly 12% compared to the same period last year, and active inventory — the total number of homes available at any given moment — is climbing for the first time since 2022.

That's meaningful. Here's what it actually means for buyers in the market right now.

## What the numbers show

The metro-wide numbers are encouraging, but they're not evenly distributed. The inventory increase is concentrated in the outer suburbs and higher price points. If you're shopping in Crocus Hill, Mac-Groveland, or Linden Hills under $700k, you're still operating in a seller's market. Multiple offers remain common on well-priced, well-presented homes.

Move out to Woodbury, Maple Grove, or the western suburbs, and the picture is different. Days on market are up. Price reductions are appearing. Buyers have room to negotiate in ways they simply didn't six months ago.

## What it means for your strategy

**If you're buying in the urban core:** The inventory bump helps at the margins — you'll have slightly more to look at — but don't expect the competitive pressure to ease dramatically. Get your financing locked down, know your number, and be ready to move when the right property appears.

**If you're buying in the suburbs:** You're in a stronger negotiating position than buyers have been in years. Use it. Ask for closing cost contributions. Request an inspection contingency. Don't waive things you shouldn't waive just because you got used to doing that during the frenzy.

**If you've been waiting for the market to "crash":** It hasn't, and the fundamentals don't suggest it will. What you're seeing is normalization — a healthier market, not a buyer's bonanza. Waiting longer means waiting through more rate uncertainty, not less competition.

## The rate question

Inventory improving is good news. Rates staying elevated is the counterweight. At current rates, every $100,000 of purchase price is roughly $650/month in principal and interest. That math is real and it affects what you can afford.

The buyers doing well right now are the ones who have accepted the rate environment, underwritten their purchase at current rates rather than a hoped-for future rate, and focused on finding the right house at the right price — not the perfect conditions that may never arrive.

## Bottom line

More inventory is unambiguously good for buyers. Use the additional selection to be more deliberate. Don't let a slightly less frenzied market make you complacent about preparation — financing, inspection strategy, and offer structure still matter enormously.
